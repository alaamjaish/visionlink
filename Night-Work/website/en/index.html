The problem

The factory floor in 2026 still runs on memory and paper.

Three failure modes keep happening, every shift, in every facility we asked about.

01

Knowledge walks out the door.

When a senior technician retires, their procedural know-how leaves with them. The next worker has to relearn it from scratch.

02

Emergencies are slow and confused.

An injured worker can't unlock a phone or open an app. Help arrives later than it should, with worse information than it should.

03

Reporting never reaches anyone.

Incidents and parts requests get scribbled on paper or skipped entirely. The analytics layer never sees the floor.

What it is

One device on the worker.
One screen for the supervisor.
Six buttons in between.

A Raspberry Pi 4B in a 3D-printed shell. A Pi Camera 3. An I²S microphone, an I²S amplifier, a 1 W speaker, and six tactile push buttons. A 10 000 mAh power bank.

When the worker presses a button, the Pi opens a streaming session with Google Gemini or OpenAI Realtime, calls the matching tool, writes to Postgres, sends an email, captures a frame — whatever the gesture asked for. The supervisor sees it appear on their dashboard in under a quarter-second.

The interface

Six buttons. Two modes. One device.

Three handle documentation. Two run the AI agent. One is dedicated to emergencies. Every press is a falling-edge GPIO interrupt that dispatches to one async handler.

B1 · GPIO 23
Documentation Session
Open a shift session in Supabase. Everything captured while it's open is grouped under the same session_id.
Single — open Single again — close
B2 · GPIO 25
Photo / Video
Single press snaps a 1280×720 JPEG. Double press records a 5-second MP4. Both upload to storage and appear on the dashboard.
Single — photo Double — 5s video
B3 · GPIO 22
Voice Note
Press to record, press again to upload. PCM 16 kHz from the I²S mic, wrapped in a WAV header.
Press → record Press → upload
B4 · GPIO 5
AI Voice Agent
A bidirectional voice session with Gemini Live or OpenAI Realtime. The AI hears, replies, and calls six live tools.
Single — start Double — stop
B5 · GPIO 6
AI Voice + Vision
Same as B4, with the camera attached. Each press injects a fresh JPEG into the conversation — the AI sees what the worker sees.
Single — start / snap Double — stop
B6 · GPIO 13 · SOS
Panic Mode
Double-press arms an SOS. Emails the safety officer, opens a calm AI conversation, auto-snaps a frame every 10 s, supervisor can shut off in ~2 s.
Single — warning Double — armed

The other half

While the worker presses, the supervisor sees.

Eleven realtime panels on a Next.js 16 dashboard. Every incident, every capture, every email — pushed over Supabase Realtime in ~200 ms. The SOS panel turns red the moment B6 fires.

/ Command Center

Nine live panels.

SOS · Wearable Settings · Incidents · Tasks · Parts · Components · Managers · Report Templates · Sent Reports. Full CRUD with optimistic updates and a 1.4 s flash-in animation on every change.

/documentation

Every shift, captured.

A stats strip on top. Sessions panel below. Captures grid tabbed by kind — All, Photos, Videos, Voice notes, Orphans. Click any row to expand.
